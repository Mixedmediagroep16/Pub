RUDOLF LABAN

Rudolf Laban was a dance artist & theorist. He is notable as one of the pioneers of modern dance in Europe. He created the Laban Movement Analysis. It is a method and language for describing, visualizing, interpreting and documenting all varieties of human movement. It uses a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating contributions from anatomy, kinesiology, psychology and other fields.


LABAN NOTATION

The dance space is formed by a moving body, which is always in the middle.

This middle is surrounded by the kinesphere, a spherical shape, which is formed by the reaching of a limb from the first position.

The position is the projection of the perpendicular from the center of gravity of the body and is embodied by one foot, which is fixed in one position of kinesphere on one spot in the room.

If the dancer takes a step with one foot and then the other then there will be a new kinesphere a new position in space.

We can imagine a cube circumscribing the kinesphere. On this circumscribing cube are projected coordinates which indicate the direction to which the dancing extends with a limb. A succession of such coordinates goes on to describe a movement on the surface, or in the space of the kinesphere.

From the center of gravity of the body Laban constructs a system of diagonals through the coordination of the cube.
